# Future Herd

> Our food system is the result of a wide range of decisions that are made by people and producers throughout the agricultural and food supply chain. A society that neither understands these decisions nor has the ability to participate in them is not going to be healthy or democratic. Civic literacy and food literacy are inseparable and part of the same cultural foundation for any aspiring democratic society.

Future Herd is modern participation infrastructure for agricultural organizations and rural communities. Open source. Agent-assisted. Built for the realities of rural connectivity and the speed of today's information environment.

It draws on [Decidim](https://decidim.org/) for structured participatory governance and [Polis](https://pol.is/) for opinion-mapping through dimensionality reduction, and extends them with an AI agent layer, a peer-driven trust and standing system, and deep ecosystem integration. Future Herd is not a social network, not a forum. It is structured decision-making infrastructure — designed to give agricultural organizations and the communities around them a way to surface what people actually think, deliberate openly, and produce documented outcomes.

---

## Design Principles

**Open participation with identity integrity.** Anyone affected by agricultural decisions can participate. No one can participate twice. Organizational affiliation is visible and meaningful — it shapes how contributions are weighted and read — but it is not a gate. Participation is not reserved for members.

**Transparency by default.** Every agent action is logged and auditable. Every deliberation history is preserved. Participation data flows to leadership as structured insight, not filtered summaries. The platform does not make decisions on behalf of anyone — it makes the basis for decisions visible.

**Peer-driven trust and standing.** An alternative path to standing that is open to anyone, based on peer recognition of quality contributions — not volume, not institutional position. This is not a reputation score. It is a record of how the community itself has recognized your participation over time.

**Ecosystem integration from day one.** The platform connects to where conversations already happen — social media, podcasts, newsletters, news — rather than trying to replace them. Every output is shareable. Every input can come from anywhere. The platform earns its place by being useful to the conversations that already exist, not by demanding people come to it.

**Rural-first infrastructure.** Offline-capable. Channel-agnostic messaging. Low-bandwidth by design. Tested on the devices and connections people actually have. Agricultural decisions happen in places with intermittent connectivity, and the platform has to work there.

**Strengthens authority by making it measurable.** The platform does not replace boards, elections, or formal governance structures. It gives them better information and documented mandates. When a board makes a decision, they can point to the deliberative record. When they depart from emerging consensus, they can explain why.

## Project Status

Future Herd is in the documentation and architecture phase. No code has been written yet. The work right now is establishing the decisions and designs that will make the code worth writing.

The technical roadmap is in [docs/roadmap.md](docs/roadmap.md). Architecture decision records are in [docs/architecture/](docs/architecture/). Design documents are in [docs/design/](docs/design/).

Contributions to architecture decisions, design documents, and research are welcome now, before a line of code exists. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).
